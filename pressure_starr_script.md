# TRAP DADDY SERIES — PRESSURE

**Scene 1: EXT. DOWNTOWN ATLANTA PARKING GARAGE - NIGHT**
The top level of a concrete parking garage overlooking the glowing Atlanta skyline. Rain slicks the pavement. A black SUV idles in the shadows, headlights off. A large digital billboard in the background flashes the time: 11:58 PM. The numbers glow bright red, reflecting in the puddles. A man in a dark hoodie stands near the edge, leaning against the concrete wall. He checks his watch, tense.

> **NARRATOR (V.O.)**
> In this city, power doesn't announce itself. It arrives quietly, usually when you're out of options. And time is the one thing you can't buy back.

---

**Scene 2: INT. BLACK SUV - CONTINUOUS**
Inside the SUV, the atmosphere is heavy. GREGORY STARR sits in the back seat. He is a Black man wearing a sharp, tailored charcoal suit and a classic fedora hat that casts a shadow over his eyes, concealing his identity. He taps a silver ring against the leather armrest rhythmically. On his wrist, an expensive mechanical watch ticks loudly.

> **GREGORY STARR**
> *(Deep, calm, authoritative)*
> Let him wait. The rain builds character. We have two minutes.

---

**Scene 3: EXT. DOWNTOWN ATLANTA PARKING GARAGE - NIGHT**
The back window of the SUV rolls down slowly. The man in the hoodie turns around, walking toward the vehicle. He stops a few feet away, rain dripping from his hood. The digital billboard behind him flips to 11:59 PM. The red light washes over his face.

> **MAN IN HOODIE**
> *(Frustrated, trying to sound tough)*
> I told you I'd have it. You didn't have to bring the muscle.

> **GREGORY STARR (V.O.)**
> I didn't bring muscle. I brought a reminder.

---

**Scene 4: INT/EXT. SUV WINDOW - CONTINUOUS**
Gregory Starr leans forward slightly, just enough for the streetlights to catch the sharp lapel of his suit, but his face remains hidden in the shadow of the fedora. Smoke drifts out the window. The ticking of his watch seems to echo in the confined space of the car.

> **GREGORY STARR**
> *(Cold, precise)*
> You have sixty seconds to hand over the ledger. Or the deal expires, and my associates handle the collection their way.

---

**Scene 5: EXT. DOWNTOWN ATLANTA PARKING GARAGE - NIGHT**
The man in the hoodie swallows hard, looking away for a second before looking back at the dark window. He knows Starr is right. The billboard flashes: 12:00 AM. Midnight. He reaches into his jacket slowly, pulling out a thick manila envelope, and holds it out in the rain.

> **MAN IN HOODIE**
> *(Defeated but firm)*
> Here's the ledger. Every name, every drop. We're done.

---

**Scene 6: EXT. DOWNTOWN ATLANTA PARKING GARAGE - NIGHT**
A large, gloved hand reaches out from the dark window and takes the envelope. The window begins to roll up immediately. The SUV's engine revs, headlights flicking on and blinding the man for a second. The red numbers of the clock reflect on the wet pavement as the SUV pulls away.

> **GREGORY STARR (V.O.)**
> We have a beginning. Don't call me again. I'll find you.

> **NARRATOR (V.O.)**
> He had the money he needed, but he knew he just sold something he could never buy back. The clock was always ticking.
