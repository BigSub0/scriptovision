# TRAP DADDY SERIES — PILOT: MEMORIAL DAY '81

**Scene 1: EXT. ROSELAND LITTLE LEAGUE FIELD - DAY**
A dusty baseball diamond in Roseland, Chicago. The scoreboard shows the home team losing by one run in the bottom of the last inning. The sun beats down on the dirt. MALIK (14, tall, slim) and ANDRE (15, shorter but older) stand near the dugout. They wear matching yellow little league uniforms with green writing across the chest. Malik looks devastated, staring at the scoreboard. Andre kicks the dirt in frustration.

> **NARRATOR (V.O.)**
> Memorial Day, 1981. The day we lost the Roseland Little League Championship. But that wasn't the loss that changed everything.

---

**Scene 2: EXT. ROSELAND STREETS - LATER THAT DAY**
Malik and Andre walk down a cracked sidewalk, still in their yellow and green uniforms. The neighborhood is alive with summer energy, but their mood is heavy. Andre spots a shiny, unattended bicycle leaning against a chain-link fence outside a corner store. He nudges Malik and points.

> **ANDRE**
> *(Frustrated, rebellious)*
> Man, forget that game. Look at that. Nobody's even watching it.

> **MALIK**
> *(Hesitant but following)*
> You sure, Dre? We just lost, we don't need more trouble.

---

**Scene 3: EXT. CORNER STORE - CONTINUOUS**
Andre grabs the handlebars of the bike. Just as he swings his leg over, an OLDER MAN (40s, kind face, wearing a faded mechanic's shirt) steps out of the store. He holds up his hands peacefully, not looking for a fight.

> **OLDER MAN**
> *(Calm, reasonable)*
> Hey now, young bloods. That's not yours. Just leave it be and go on home. No harm done.

> **ANDRE**
> *(Defensive, gripping the bike)*
> We were just looking at it, old man.

---

**Scene 4: EXT. CORNER STORE - CONTINUOUS**
Suddenly, a THUG (20s, hardened, wearing a leather jacket despite the heat) steps out from the alleyway next to the store. He sees the Older Man confronting the boys. The Thug pulls a heavy revolver from his waistband, his face twisted in unprovoked anger.

> **THUG**
> *(Aggressive, shouting)*
> You got a problem with them, you got a problem with me! Back up!

> **OLDER MAN**
> *(Panicking, stepping back)*
> Whoa, son! I was just getting my bike—

---

**Scene 5: EXT. CORNER STORE - CONTINUOUS**
The Thug raises the gun and fires. The loud crack echoes off the brick buildings. The Older Man falls backward onto the pavement. Malik and Andre freeze in absolute shock, their yellow uniforms bright against the grim reality of the street. The Thug doesn't even look at the boys; he just turns and runs down the alley.

> **NARRATOR (V.O.)**
> It happened so fast. A good man, dead over a piece of metal.

---

**Scene 6: EXT. CORNER STORE - CONTINUOUS**
Malik and Andre stand frozen over the Older Man's body. The stolen bike lies forgotten on the ground next to them. Sirens begin to wail in the distance. Malik looks at Andre, the innocence completely gone from their eyes.

> **NARRATOR (V.O.)**
> We didn't pull the trigger. But standing there in those yellow uniforms, watching the blood pool on the concrete... that was the exact moment we stopped being kids. The streets had us now.
